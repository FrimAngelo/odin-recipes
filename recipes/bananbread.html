Banana Bread

Description

Banana bread is a warm and comforting classic, beloved for its moist texture, sweet flavor, and versatility. This recipe transforms overripe bananas into a delicious treat, combining their natural sweetness with pantry staples like flour, sugar, and eggs. The batter is enriched with butter or oil for tenderness and flavored with a touch of vanilla or cinnamon for warmth. As it bakes, the kitchen fills with an irresistible aroma, signaling the arrival of something truly special.

The beauty of banana bread lies in its adaptability. Add-ins like chocolate chips, nuts, or even dried fruit can elevate the loaf, making it perfect for breakfast, a snack, or dessert. Easy to prepare and forgiving for beginners, banana bread is a go-to recipe for using up overripe bananas and creating a baked good that feels as cozy as a hug. Whether enjoyed fresh from the oven or toasted with butter the next day, every bite is a reminder of how simple ingredients can create something extraordinary.

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed (about 1 1/2 cups)
  • 1/3 cup (75g) melted butter (or vegetable oil)
  • 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1Pinch of salt
  • 1 1/2 cups (190g) all-purpose flour
  • Optional: 1/2 cup (75g) chopped nuts, chocolate chips, or raisins

Steps

  1. Prepare the Batter:
    • Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan or line it with parchment paper.
    • In a large mixing bowl, mash the bananas with a fork until smooth.
    • Stir in the melted butter (or oil) until well combined
    • Mix in the sugar, beaten egg, and vanilla extract
  2. Incorporate the Dry Ingredients:
    • Sprinkle the baking soda and salt over the banana mixture, then stir to combine.
    • Gently fold in the flour until just incorporated. Avoid overmixing to keep the bread tender.
    • If using nuts, chocolate chips, or raisins, fold them into the batter
  3. Bake the Banana Bread:
    • Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and spread it evenly.
    • Bake for 50–60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
    • Let the banana bread cool in the pan for about 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.
